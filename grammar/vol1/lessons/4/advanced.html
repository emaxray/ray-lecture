Lesson 4: Articles - Advanced Homework

Challenge yourself with complex article usage

📜 Advanced Instructions

This advanced homework tests deeper understanding of articles.
Complete all sections: noun forms, articles, quantifiers, and error correction.
Be prepared to explain your reasoning in class!

Part 1: Noun Forms [01-07]

1
Transform the noun

Sometimes there are 28 _____ in February. ( day )

days

📚 Reasoning:

Plural Rule "28" = multiple → need plural
day → days

2
Transform the noun

Tolstoy wrote twelve short _____ for kids ( story )

stories

📚 Reasoning:

Plural Rule "twelve" = multiple → need plural
story → stories (y → ies after consonant)

3
Transform the noun

The main idea of the article was about _____. ( friendship )

friendship

📚 Reasoning:

Abstract Noun "friendship" = abstract/uncountable
No change needed (stays singular)

4
Transform the noun

There are two _____ in my home. ( piano )

pianos

📚 Reasoning:

Plural Rule "two" = multiple → need plural
piano → pianos (just add -s after vowel+o)

5
Transform the noun

_____ is not everything in life although it is important. ( money )

Money

📚 Reasoning:

Uncountable "money" = uncountable noun
No change, just capitalize at sentence start

6
Transform the noun

Many _____ fall from the trees in fall. ( leaf )

leaves

📚 Reasoning:

Irregular Plural "Many" = multiple → need plural
leaf → leaves (f → ves)

7
Transform the noun

Spiderman and Superman are _____ in comic books. ( hero )

heroes

📚 Reasoning:

Plural Rule Two people = plural needed
hero → heroes (add -es after consonant+o)

Part 2: Articles [08-14]

8
Choose: a / an / the / X

I can't eat anymore. I had _____ dinner 30 minutes ago.

X (no article)

📚 Reasoning:

Lazy Rule Meals (breakfast, lunch, dinner) → NO article
Plain meal without modification

9
Choose: a / an / the / X

There is _____ big tree on the street.

a

📚 Reasoning:

First Mention First time mentioning the tree
A = one random/unknown tree

10
Choose: a / an / the / X

Yesterday my dad was driving over 150km _____ hour.

an

📚 Reasoning:

Per/Each Rule A/AN = per/each (매, ~마다)
hour starts with vowel sound → AN hour

11
Choose: a / an / the / X

Did you come here by _____ taxi?

X (no article)

📚 Reasoning:

Transportation BY + transportation → NO article
by taxi, by bus, by train

12
Choose: a / an / the / X

My friend and I are going to travel around _____ world this summer.

the

📚 Reasoning:

Unique "world" = unique (only one exists)
THE = everyone knows which one

13
Choose: a / an / the / X

Our teammates have _____ opinion about the project.

an

📚 Reasoning:

Vowel Sound opinion = 오피니언 (starts with vowel sound)
AN + vowel sound

14
Choose: a / an / the / X

In Korea, in the morning _____ sun rises, and at night _____ moon rises.

the / the

📚 Reasoning:

Unique Sun and moon = unique celestial bodies
THE sun, THE moon (only one of each)

Part 3: Quantifiers [15-20]

15
Choose: many / much

I don't have _____ money now.
They didn't take _____ photos in Austria.

  • much money (uncountable)
  • many photos (countable plural)

📚 Reasoning:

Countable vs Uncountable MUCH + uncountable (money, water, time)
MANY + countable plural (photos, books, people)

16
Choose: a few / a little

Nick puts _____ milk in his coffee.
_____ children were playing on the street.

  • a little milk (uncountable)
  • A few children (countable)

📚 Reasoning:

Positive Small Amount A LITTLE + uncountable = some (positive)
A FEW + countable = some (positive)

17
Choose: few / little

Angela has _____ friends.
There is _____ food in the refrigerator.

  • few friends (countable - negative meaning)
  • little food (uncountable - negative meaning)

📚 Reasoning:

Negative Meaning FEW + countable = almost none (negative)
LITTLE + uncountable = almost none (negative)
(Without "a" = negative/not enough)

18
Choose: some / any

There are _____ taxis on the street.
I didn't find _____ information on the Internet.

  • some taxis (positive sentence)
  • any information (negative sentence)

📚 Reasoning:

Some vs Any SOME = positive sentences
ANY = negative sentences & questions

19
Choose: a few / a little

I'll give _____ water to these plants.
We saw Henry _____ minutes ago.

  • a little water (uncountable)
  • a few minutes (countable)

📚 Reasoning:

Small Positive Amount A LITTLE = some (uncountable)
A FEW = some (countable plural)

20
Choose: few / little

_____ people know about his novels.
Mandy has _____ experience in the field.

  • Few people (almost nobody knows)
  • little experience (almost no experience)

📚 Reasoning:

Negative Emphasis Without "a" = emphasizes lack/shortage
Few = not many / Little = not much

Part 4: Error Correction [21-30]

21
Find the incorrect sentence

다음 문장 중 어법상 어색한 것은?

① David goes to school by bus.
② We played football in the park.
③ I had a lunch with my friend.
④ My sister plays the piano every day.
⑤ I drink some milk and ate apples in the morning.

③ I had a lunch with my friend.

📚 Reasoning:

Meal Rule Error: "a lunch" → should be "lunch" (no article)
Meals without modifiers = NO article
Correct: "I had lunch with my friend."

22
Find the incorrect sentence

다음 중 어법상 틀린 것을 고르시오.

① I have a headache, too.
② I have dark circles under my eyes.
③ I'm sorry, but I don't remember its name.
④ There is a special cream for dark circles.
⑤ Lisa has some worries, so she asks for help on the web.

All sentences are correct!

📚 Reasoning:

Trick Question All sentences are grammatically correct:
① a headache ✓ (countable)
② dark circles ✓ (plural, no article)
③ its name ✓ (possessive)
④ a special cream ✓ (countable)
⑤ some worries ✓ (some + plural)

23
Find the incorrect sentence

다음 중 어법상 틀린 것을 고르시오.

① Boys, it's time to go to bed.
② This letter will be delivered by airplane.
③ He has an MP3 player.
④ Many fish are in the lake.
⑤ I'm looking for an used car.

⑤ I'm looking for an used car.

📚 Reasoning:

Special 유 Sound Error: "an used" → should be "a used"
used = 유즈드 (starts with ㅠ sound = consonant)
Correct: "I'm looking for a used car."

24
Find the incorrect pair

다음 명사의 단수형과 복수형이 바르게 연결되지 않은 것은?

① goose - gooses
② foot - feet
③ mouse - mice
④ child - children
⑤ man - men

① goose - gooses

📚 Reasoning:

Irregular Plural Error: goose → gooses ✗
Correct: goose → geese ✓
All others are correct irregular plurals

25
Find the incorrect plural

다음 명사의 복수형이 잘못 짝지어진 것을 고르시오.

① leaf - leaves
② fly - flies
③ thief - thiefs
④ roof - roofs
⑤ tooth - teeth

③ thief - thiefs

📚 Reasoning:

F → VES Rule Error: thief → thiefs ✗
Correct: thief → thieves ✓
Rule: f/fe → ves (except roof, chief)

26
Fill in the blanks

I think she doesn't have ________ money.
I'd like __________ hot water, please.

any / some

📚 Reasoning:

Some vs Any 1st: negative sentence → ANY
2nd: polite request → SOME (even in question)

27
Find the incorrect sentence

다음 밑줄 친 부분이 어법상 어색한 것은?

① We need food, water and air to live.
② Sam is from Chicago, Illinois.
③ Everyone wants peace and freedom.
④ He gave me some advices.
⑤ My father has little knowledge of cooking.

④ He gave me some advices.

📚 Reasoning:

Uncountable Noun Error: "advices" → "advice" is uncountable
Never add -s to uncountable nouns
Correct: "He gave me some advice."

28
Find the incorrect sentence

다음 밑줄 친 부분이 잘못된 것을 고르시오.

① I will buy some clothes.
② Do you have any hobbies?
③ There are some cups on the table.
④ Andrew doesn't have some friends.
⑤ We prepared some sandwiches for the trip.

④ Andrew doesn't have some friends.

📚 Reasoning:

Negative + Any Error: "doesn't have some" → "doesn't have any"
Negative sentences require ANY, not SOME
Correct: "Andrew doesn't have any friends."

29
Fill in the blank

The man drank three ______________ of water.

bottles

📚 Reasoning:

Measure Words "three" = plural needed
bottle → bottles (measure word for liquids)
Pattern: number + plural measure word + of + noun

30
Choose the correct word

I don't have _____ ideas.

any

📚 Reasoning:

Negative Rule "don't have" = negative sentence
Negative + ANY (not some)
ideas = countable plural

🎯 Advanced Summary

Key Advanced Patterns to Remember:

  • Irregular Plurals: man→men, woman→women, child→children, foot→feet, tooth→teeth, mouse→mice, goose→geese
  • F/FE → VES: leaf→leaves, thief→thieves, wife→wives (BUT: roof→roofs, chief→chiefs)
  • Quantifiers:
    • much/many (negative/questions)
    • a little/a few (positive small amount)
    • little/few (negative - almost none)
  • Some vs Any: Some (positive/requests), Any (negative/questions)
  • Uncountable Nouns: advice, information, news, money, furniture (NO plural -s)
