Lesson 4: Articles - Basic Homework

Master the art of a, an, and the

📜 Instructions

Complete the homework at home. Answers and reasoning are provided.
Be ready to explain your answers verbally in class!
Remember: Articles are used with SINGULAR COUNTABLE NOUNS!

1

I found _____ book that I once had read.

the

📚 Reasoning:

Rule ② "that I once had read" tells us this is a SPECIFIC book - we know which one!
THE = specific/known (like a pronoun)

3

She bought _____ scarf in the department store.

a

📚 Reasoning:

First mention First time mentioning the scarf = unknown/random
A = ONE + RANDOM (we don't know which scarf yet)

5

We have to go straight in _____ west to find a land.

the

📚 Reasoning:

Rule ① Directions (north, south, east, west) are UNIQUE - only one west exists!
THE = unique things everyone knows

7

The poor _____ to be cared for by our government.

have

📚 Reasoning:

Rule ⑤ THE + adjective = plural people
"The poor" = poor people (plural) → use HAVE not HAS

9

We have _____ same reason to meet him.

the

📚 Reasoning:

Rule ④ "same" = only one exists
Words like same, only, first, last, best → always use THE

11

_____ my teacher asked me not to be late.

X (no article)

📚 Reasoning:

Possessive Rule "my" is a possessive → NO article before possessives
Never: "the my" or "a my" - it's just "my teacher"

13

We can discuss the issue before _____ dinner.

X (no article)

📚 Reasoning:

Lazy Rule ② Meals (breakfast, lunch, dinner) → NO article
People are lazy with daily meals!

15

We have to go there by _____ taxi in order to be on time.

X (no article)

📚 Reasoning:

Transportation Rule BY + transportation → NO article
by taxi, by bus, by car (but: in a taxi, on the bus)

17

I'm so full with _____ heavy breakfast.

a

📚 Reasoning:

Modified Meal "heavy" modifies "breakfast" → lazy rule DOESN'T apply
With adjectives: a heavy breakfast, a delicious lunch

19

I will go to a world trip with _____ bicycle.

a

📚 Reasoning:

Preposition Difference WITH bicycle (not BY) → need article
A = one random bicycle

21

There is _____ American cultural center in my town.

an

📚 Reasoning:

A vs AN American = 어메리칸 (starts with vowel sound)
Use AN before vowel sounds

23

Tony was chosen to be _____ MVP by newspaper reporters.

an

📚 Reasoning:

Pronunciation Rule MVP = "em-vee-pee" (starts with 엠)
Vowel sound → use AN

25

Tony was chosen to be _____ most valuable player by newspaper reporters.

the

📚 Reasoning:

Rule ④ "most" = superlative → always use THE
the best, the worst, the most valuable

27

This is _____ news to me.

X (no article)

📚 Reasoning:

Uncountable "news" is UNCOUNTABLE (no singular/plural)
Articles only with SINGULAR COUNTABLE nouns

29

_____ Ms. Park called you this morning.

A

📚 Reasoning:

Rule ③ A + name = "어떤" (some/unknown person)
"A Ms. Park" = some Ms. Park (we don't know which one)

2

Would you mind opening _____ window?

the

📚 Reasoning:

Rule ③ Context makes it clear - we both know which window
THE = specific (probably pointing or obvious from context)

4

He started playing _____ violin in front of people.

the

📚 Reasoning:

Rule ④ Musical instruments → always use THE
play the piano, play the guitar, play the violin

6

Our office is on _____ first floor of the building.

the

📚 Reasoning:

Ordinal Numbers Ordinal numbers (first, second, third) → always use THE
Only one "first floor" exists in a building

8

He caught her by _____ wrist.

the

📚 Reasoning:

Body Parts Body parts with "by" → use THE
by the hand, by the arm (specific body part)

10

I enjoyed playing _____ baseball in weekend.

X (no article)

📚 Reasoning:

Lazy Rule ① Sports → NO article
play basketball, play soccer, play baseball

12

_____ woman called Sally visited your house.

A

📚 Reasoning:

First Mention Introducing Sally for the first time
A = one unknown woman (named Sally)

14

Our kids always goes to _____ school at 7 am.

X (no article)

📚 Reasoning:

Lazy Rule ④ "go to school" = activity/purpose (studying)
vs "go to the school" = specific building

16

Hey, Sam, let's get out and play _____ baseball.

X (no article)

📚 Reasoning:

Lazy Rule ① Sports → NO article
Same as question 10: play baseball

18

Can you tell me how to get to _____ school?

the

📚 Reasoning:

Specific Building Asking for directions → specific school building
THE = the school we both know about

20

Stop watching TV and go to _____ bed.

X (no article)

📚 Reasoning:

Lazy Rule ④ "go to bed" = activity (sleeping)
Common daily activities → NO article

22

I need to wear _____ uniform to go to school.

a

📚 Reasoning:

Special 유 Sound uniform = 유니폼 (starts with ㅠ sound)
유 sound = consonant → use A not AN

24

He has _____ useful tool to fix the broken door.

a

📚 Reasoning:

Special 유 Sound useful = 유스풀 (starts with ㅠ sound)
유 sound = consonant → use A not AN

26

In this special desert there is _____ water.

X (no article)

📚 Reasoning:

Uncountable "water" is UNCOUNTABLE
Articles only with SINGULAR COUNTABLE nouns

28

I have _____ eight-year-old brothers.

X (no article)

📚 Reasoning:

Plural "brothers" is PLURAL
Articles only with SINGULAR countable nouns

30

Kate takes a painting lesson once _____ week.

a

📚 Reasoning:

Rule ② A = per/each (매, ~마다)
once a week, twice a day, three times a year

📚 Extra Review Questions

Why these questions?
These are the MOST CONFUSING questions students often get wrong!

Study these explanations carefully to understand:
• Common mistakes and how to avoid them
• Special rules that aren't obvious
• Tricky patterns in English articles

📖 Extra Study for Odd Numbers Students

7

⚠️ Why tricky? Students often write "has" because they think "the poor" is singular!

The poor _____ to be cared for by our government.

have

🎯 Advanced Analysis:

1) THE + adjective = plural people group
2) "The poor" = "Poor people" (plural)
3) Similar examples: the rich, the young, the elderly
4) Always use plural verb (have, are, were)

Memory Trick: "The poor" = "They" not "It"!

11

⚠️ Why tricky? Students want to add "The" before "my"!

_____ my teacher asked me not to be late.

X (no article)

🎯 Advanced Analysis:

1) Possessives (my, your, his, her) block articles
2) Never: "the my" or "a my" - grammatically impossible
3) Without "my": "The teacher asked me..." ✓
4) One determiner rule: only one of (article/possessive/demonstrative)

Remember: MY already points to which teacher!

17

⚠️ Why tricky? Students remember "no article with meals" but forget adjectives change the rule!

I'm so full with _____ heavy breakfast.

a

🎯 Advanced Analysis:

1) Plain meal: "eat breakfast" ✓ (lazy rule)
2) Modified meal: "eat a heavy breakfast" ✓
3) Adjectives break the lazy rule!
4) Other examples: a quick lunch, a delicious dinner

Rule: Adjective + meal = need article!

23

⚠️ Why tricky? Students see "M" and think consonant = "a"!

Tony was chosen to be _____ MVP by newspaper reporters.

an

🎯 Advanced Analysis:

1) MVP = "em-vee-pee" (엠브이피)
2) Starts with vowel sound (엠) → AN
3) Other examples: an FBI agent, an NBA player
4) But: a CEO (씨이오 - starts with ㅆ)

Tip: Say it in Korean first!

29

⚠️ Why tricky? Students think names never need articles!

_____ Ms. Park called you this morning.

A

🎯 Advanced Analysis:

1) "A + name" = some unknown person with that name
2) Translation: "어떤 박 선생님이" (some Ms. Park)
3) vs "Ms. Park called" = the Ms. Park we know
4) Used when: multiple people same name, unknown caller

Context: The speaker doesn't know this Ms. Park!

📖 Extra Study for Even Numbers Students

4

⚠️ Why tricky? Students forget musical instruments ALWAYS use "the"!

He started playing _____ violin in front of people.

the

🎯 Advanced Analysis:

1) ALL musical instruments use THE
2) play the piano, the guitar, the drums
3) Exception: when counting ("I bought a piano")
4) Rule applies even with modifiers: play the electric guitar

No exceptions: Even "play the triangle"!

10

⚠️ Why tricky? Some students add "the" thinking it's a specific sport!

I enjoyed playing _____ baseball in weekend.

X (no article)

🎯 Advanced Analysis:

1) ALL sports: NO article (lazy rule)
2) play tennis, play golf, play cricket
3) Exception: "play a game of baseball" (not the sport itself)
4) Also: do yoga, do karate (activities)

Remember: Sports = daily activity = lazy rule!

14 vs 18

⚠️ Why tricky? Same word "school" but different meanings = different articles!

Compare: "go to _____ school" vs "get to _____ school"

X (no article) vs the

🎯 Advanced Analysis:

1) "go to school" = purpose/activity (studying) → NO article
2) "get to the school" = physical building → THE
3) Similar: go to church (worship) vs go to the church (building)
4) Context changes everything!

Key: Activity vs Building!

22 & 24

⚠️ Why tricky? Students see "u" and think vowel = "an"!

The special "유" sound: uniform, useful, university

All use "a" not "an"

🎯 Advanced Analysis:

1) These words start with 유 (yu) sound, not 우 (u)
2) 유 = consonant sound (like "yes")
3) a uniform, a useful tool, a university
4) Compare: an umbrella (엄브렐라 - vowel sound)

Test: Say it in Korean - does it start with ㅇ or ㅠ?

27 & 28

⚠️ Why tricky? Students forget to check if it's countable AND singular!

Why no article? "This is _____ news" & "I have _____ brothers"

X (no article) for both

🎯 Advanced Analysis:

1) Articles ONLY with SINGULAR + COUNTABLE
2) "news" = uncountable (like water, advice, information)
3) "brothers" = plural (not singular)
4) Remember: ONE thing that we can count!

Check both: Is it ONE? Can we count it?

✍️ Summary Homework

Create Your Own Compound Sentences

Requirements:

📝
Write 5 COMPOUND SENTENCES total
Each sentence must be at least 14 words long
Use connecting words like: and, but, so, because, when, after, before
✓
Include ALL article types in your 5 sentences:
• At least one sentence with "A/AN"
• At least one sentence with "THE"
• At least one sentence with NO article (lazy rule or uncountable/plural)
⭐
Bonus Challenge:
Try to include different article rules we learned
(musical instruments, sports, meals, unique things, etc.)

Example Compound Sentences:

1. "I saw an elephant at the zoo yesterday, and it was eating ∅ peanuts from visitors' hands."
(AN + vowel sound, NO article with plural)

2. "My sister plays the piano beautifully, but she never wants to perform in ∅ public concerts."
(THE + musical instrument, NO article with abstract noun)

3. "We had a delicious dinner at the new restaurant, so we decided to go there again."
(A + adjective + meal breaks lazy rule)

📏 Length Check Tip

Count your words! Each sentence needs 14+ words.
Use Word Count in your computer or count by hand.
Short sentence? Add details with "because," "when," or "and"!

📓 Write your 5 sentences in your notebook
🗣️ Practice reading them aloud
💡 Be ready to explain your article choices in class!
