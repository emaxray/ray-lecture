Lesson 5: Pronouns - Basic Homework

Master the art of pronouns and their usage

📜 Instructions

Complete the homework at home. Answers and reasoning are provided.
Be ready to explain your answers verbally in class!
Focus on understanding WHY each answer is correct.

2

I don't like this case. Please show me _____.

another

📚 Reasoning:

Unknown total cases = indefinite
ANOTHER = different one (unknown which one)

4

We know _____ very well.

each other

📚 Reasoning:

Two people, reciprocal relationship
EACH OTHER = between two people only

6

Why are the green _____ cheaper?

ones

📚 Reasoning:

Plural pronoun replacing plural noun
ONES = plural of 'one' (the green items)

8

When people get in trouble, they should help _____.

one another

📚 Reasoning:

Multiple people helping reciprocally
ONE ANOTHER = between three or more people

10

I need the camera you have. Can you lend _____ to me?

it

📚 Reasoning:

Specific camera mentioned = use "it"
IT = refers to the specific camera

12

We won't go out. It is raining.

Identify the usage of "it":

비인칭 주어 (Impersonal Subject)

📚 Reasoning:

Weather phenomenon
IT = impersonal subject for weather/time/distance

14

She likes it so don't lose it.

Identify the usage of "it":

대명사 (Pronoun)

📚 Reasoning:

Refers to specific object
IT = replacing a previously mentioned thing

16

It is seven o'clock.

Identify the usage of "it":

비인칭 주어 (Impersonal Subject)

📚 Reasoning:

Time expression
IT = impersonal subject for time

18

That toy looks fancy. It must be expensive.

Identify the usage of "it":

대명사 (Pronoun)

📚 Reasoning:

Refers to "that toy"
IT = replacing the mentioned toy

20

It is getting dark.

Identify the usage of "it":

비인칭 주어 (Impersonal Subject)

📚 Reasoning:

Natural phenomenon
IT = impersonal subject for general conditions

22

Some of the bread _____ rotten.

is

📚 Reasoning:

Bread = uncountable, singular verb
SOME OF + uncountable noun = singular

24

I don't have _____ books to read.

any

📚 Reasoning:

Negative sentence = use "any"
ANY = negative/interrogative context

26

Thomas didn't like the singer, nor do Adam. (Find the error)

do → does

📚 Reasoning:

Adam = singular subject
Should be "nor does Adam" (3rd person singular)

28

I want to hear neither pop song and classical music. (Find the error)

and → nor

📚 Reasoning:

neither...nor pattern
NEITHER always pairs with NOR, not AND

30

Your son is not a baby anymore, nor do your daughter. (Find the error)

do → is

📚 Reasoning:

Matching the main verb "is"
Should be "nor is your daughter"

📚 Additional Homework

Complete These Tasks

Required Assignments:

1
Research Assignment: "A number of" vs "The number of"
Find the difference and explain which takes singular/plural verbs.
Write a clear explanation with 2 example sentences for each.
2
Rewriting Practice
Original: Thomas didn't like the singer, nor do Adam.
• Correct the error
• Rewrite this sentence in 3 different ways (maintaining the same meaning)
3
Compound Sentences (6 total)
Write 2 sentences each using BOTH, EITHER, NEITHER
• Each sentence must be 10+ words
• Use Week 1-2 vocabulary
• Underline the pronoun in each sentence

💡 Study Tips

• Review all pronoun rules from class
• Practice identifying "it" usage in your reading
• Remember: each other (2) vs one another (3+)
• Check subject-verb agreement with compound subjects
