Chapter Summary

Chapter 10: The Tuck Cottage

  • Winnie is amazed by the messy, comfortable Tuck home - so different from her orderly life

  • Winnie's wonder turns to homesickness during the awkward meal

Vocabulary Context Clues Challenge

Read each sentence from the novel. Use context clues to select the correct definition.

1. indomitable
"The Foster women had made a fortress out of duty. Within it, they were indomitable."
a) Easily defeated or discouraged
b) Impossible to defeat or subdue
c) Very messy and disorganized
d) Extremely tired and weak
2. perilous
"There was an open cabinet where dishes were stacked in perilous towers without the least regard for their varying dimensions."
a) Perfectly organized
b) Full of danger or risk
c) Very colorful
d) Extremely tall
3. revolutionary
"And this was followed by another thought, far more revolutionary: 'Maybe they just don't care!'"
a) Spinning in circles
b) Very old and traditional
c) Involving or causing complete change
d) Extremely boring
4. elation
"And in the silence, given time to think, Winnie felt her elation, and her thoughtless pleasure, wobble and collapse."
a) Great happiness and excitement
b) Deep sadness and grief
c) Extreme anger
d) Complete confusion
5. cavernous
"Beyond this was the bedroom... opposite its foot a cavernous oak wardrobe."
a) Very small and cramped
b) Brightly colored
c) Like a large cave; vast and hollow
d) Made of expensive materials
Answer Key: 1-b, 2-b, 3-c, 4-a, 5-c

Essential Quote - Chapter 10

"Life's got to be lived, no matter how long or short... We just go along, like everybody else, one day at a time."

Speaker: Mae Tuck

Chapter: 10

Why This Quote Matters

This quote reveals the central paradox of the Tucks' immortality - they must pretend to live normally despite being frozen in time. It shows Mae's practical wisdom and highlights the burden of their eternal life.

×

Essential Quote - Chapter 11

"The pond's got answers. Come along, child. Let's go out on the water."

Speaker: Angus Tuck

Chapter: 11

Why This Quote Matters

This line sets up the crucial philosophical conversation in Chapter 12 and shows Tuck taking responsibility for explaining their situation to Winnie. The pond becomes a symbol of life's flow and the natural cycle that the Tucks have been removed from.

Preview of What's Coming Next

Tuck will take Winnie out on the pond for a crucial conversation about life, death, and the wheel of existence. This philosophical discussion will be the heart of the novel's message about mortality.
